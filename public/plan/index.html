Put the big rocks in first

There is an old picture of a jar. If you fill it first with sand and small pebbles, the big rocks never fit. But put the big rocks in first, and the sand and pebbles settle into the space around them. The jar holds everything.

Time in God's Word is a big rock. Left to the leftover minutes of a day, it is the first thing crowded out by sand. So this plan does one simple thing: it puts the rock in first. It places your time in Scripture on your calendar, at hours you choose, before the day fills up. Not another thing to feel behind on. A fixed point the rest of your life can order itself around.

The aim is not to finish a book. It is to be formed by One. Reading the whole Bible is simply what happens along the way, when you keep showing up to the fountain.

Why the number four matters

This is not only a good idea; it is a measured one. Over eight years the Center for Bible Engagement studied more than a hundred thousand people, asking a plain question: what difference does time in the Bible actually make?

The answer surprised even the researchers. Reading Scripture once, twice, even three times a week made almost no measurable difference in a person's life. The line stayed flat. But at four times a week, something changed sharply.

At four or more days a week, the effect spiked: markedly less given to anger, addiction, and despair, markedly more likely to share their faith and to grow. They named it the Power of 4, and called frequent time in the Word the single strongest predictor of spiritual growth.

Center for Bible Engagement, Understanding the Bible Engagement Challenge: Scientific Evidence for the Power of 4 (2009), now the research arm of Our Daily Bread Ministries.

Three days a week leaves you roughly where you started. Four days a week changes you. Every plan below is built to carry you past that line without your having to think about it, because the rhythm is already on your calendar.
